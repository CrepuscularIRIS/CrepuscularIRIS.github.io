# UnHiPPO: Uncertainty-aware Initialization for State Space Models

**Original Title:** UnHiPPO: Uncertainty-aware Initialization for State Space Models  
**Authors:** Marten Lienen, Abdullah Saydemir, Stephan Günnemann  
**Published:** ICLR 2025 | **Link:** [OpenReview](https://openreview.net/forum?id=U8GUmxnzXn)

---

## One-Line Summary

HiPPO — the initialization scheme powering modern state-space models like Mamba — breaks down on real noisy data. UnHiPPO patches this gap by re-deriving the same initialization from a stochastic-control perspective, making SSMs robust to noise **without adding a single FLOP at runtime**.

---

## Why This Is Worth Your Time

Most SSM papers chase architectural novelty. This one asks a quieter, harder question: *what if the foundation itself is broken?*

HiPPO (High-Order Polynomial Projection) is the initialization theory behind Mamba, S4, and a dozen follow-ons. It assumes your input sequence is a clean, noise-free signal — an assumption that holds exactly never in production.

Sensor drift, quantization artifacts, lossy compression, pre-processing pipelines: every one of these introduces noise that HiPPO treats as signal.

The authors don't just flag this problem. They solve it at the **initializer level**, which means it costs you nothing to deploy. That's the real trick here.

---

## Core Method (3-Minute Version)

HiPPO's original derivation treats the memorization of a sequence as a control problem: find a linear system whose hidden state best tracks the input as time progresses. The data enters as a *control signal* — assumed clean.

UnHiPPO flips this. It reformulates the same problem so that the data are **noisy observations of an unknown latent system**.

Now you're not just tracking a signal; you're trying to infer the underlying state from corrupted measurements.

Rather than sampling from that posterior (expensive), UnHiPPO derives a *deterministic approximation* — a single fixed initialization that already bakes in uncertainty awareness.

Plug it in where you would plug in HiPPO, and your SSM starts with a better prior for noisy regimes.

---

## Key Results

- Derived uncertainty-aware dynamics initialization by extending HiPPO theory to account for measurement noise.
- Reformulated HiPPO as a linear stochastic control problem where data are noisy outputs of a latent system.
- Demonstrated improved noise resistance at **both training and inference time** without increasing runtime.
- No concrete numbers reported in the source — experiments are presented qualitatively and on synthetic setups.

---

## Limits and Unanswered Questions

The paper is theoretically elegant, but the empirical section is thin on real-world benchmarks. The noise experiments use synthetic corruption, which raises questions about whether the gains transfer to domain-specific noise (e.g., medical sensor drift, ASR artifacts).

There's also no ablation on *how much* noise the method tolerates before degrading — a calibration curve would help practitioners know when to reach for this.

Code availability and reproducibility details are not fully specified in the current source note.
